About Me

Hello! My name is Brian. I am a generative artist.

I am originally from Houston, Texas, but came to Austin in 2009 to study at UT Austin. After completing a PhD in Nanotech Engineering in late 2019, I entered the strange landscape of 2020 where I became disenchanted with the pursuit of corporate work. I began making generative art as a pet project to improve my programming skills with the idea of becoming a developer in mind. The developer idea didn't really stick, but the generative art did.

About My Art

I am a generative artist. There are no brush (or mouse) strokes involved in my art. Instead, everything is created with algorithms, which are meticulously crafted sets of digital drawing instructions involving extensive utilization of randomness.

My work largely focuses on natural scenes and landscapes and seeks to strike a balance of rigidity and randomness that resembles the organic essence of nature. My work is unique, in part, due to my tooling - a custom built software application that hybridizes coding with the GUI and layering systems of modern digital art environments.

You can see (and use) an early iteration of this program at genartlayers.com
